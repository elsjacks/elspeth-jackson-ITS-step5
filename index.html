The Tragic Truth about BJJ

What everyone tries to hide from you about Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

Now you may of heard about the new IT sport currently making waves in the western world: Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ).
But what is this sport that involves doing laundry with people still wearing the clothes?

BJJ was created in Brazil by the Gracie family (Carlos, Oswaldo, Gastão Jr., George, and Hélio) in the early 20th century. It was adapted from Kodokan judo, brought to Brazil by Japanese judoka Mitsuyo Maeda. "Jiu-Jitsu" translates from Japanese to "gentle art" or "gentle technique".
The Brazilian martial art is a combat style that involves fighting on the ground and with grappling techniques that help you make your opponent submit to you. It is one of the most commonly used disciplines by some of the most esteemed mixed-martial arts fighters around the globe.
The principles behind the discipline are designed in a way to make the art form useful to those who have smaller body frames. The basic concept behind the art is to help people who are smaller learn how to take down bigger opponents using skill and technique rather than strength.
